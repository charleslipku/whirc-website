WHIRC research spans public health, psychology, bioengineering, and artificial
intelligence. Our work is organized around conditions that uniquely or
disproportionately affect women, and the data and methods needed to study them.

## Focus Areas

- **Maternal & Reproductive Health** — maternal mortality and morbidity,
  fertility, pregnancy outcomes, and reproductive health across the lifespan
- **Gendered Stress & Mental Health** — the health impact of gendered racism
  and intersectional stressors on women's wellbeing
- **Cardiovascular & Cellular Health** — sex differences in vascular biology
  and cardiovascular disease mechanisms
- **AI for Women's Health** — addressing bias in health AI caused by the
  underrepresentation of women in training data, and building equitable
  machine-learning tools for women's health
